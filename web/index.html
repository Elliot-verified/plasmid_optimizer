Plasmid Optimizer

Optimize DNA or amino acid sequences for bacterial expression. Generate novel peptides with PepMLM and tune novelty (top-k, temperature).

What does this tool do?

This app helps you design DNA sequences for genes you want to put in a plasmid and express in bacteria. You provide a gene or protein sequence, choose optimization constraints (organism, GC content, restriction enzymes, etc.), and get back a codon-optimized DNA sequence suitable for cloning and expression.

Step 1: Novel peptide generation (optional)

If you want to design a peptide that binds to a specific target protein, use PepMLM here. Enter the target protein sequence (or fetch it from UniProt); the model generates novel peptide sequences predicted to bind. You can copy a result and use it as your sequence in Step 2, or skip this step and paste any gene/protein sequence directly.

Step 2: Your sequence for the plasmid

Paste the amino acid or DNA sequence of the gene you want to express. This is what will be optimized: we preserve the protein sequence but change the DNA (codon choice) to match your organism and constraints.

Step 3: Constraints

Choose the expression organism (e.g. E. coli), GC content limits, restriction enzymes to avoid, and whether to avoid secondary structure and repeats. These settings guide how the DNA is optimized.

Output

Click Optimize to get plasmid-ready DNA and the corresponding amino acid sequence. Use the copy icon on any result to copy it to your clipboard.
